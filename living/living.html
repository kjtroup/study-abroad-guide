Life in Vienna

Here’s what I learned about transportation, culture, and daily living in the most livable city in the world.

Housing

I lived in OEAD Molkereistrasse. This is a student housing option offered through the government. I was randomly placed with three roommates and it was a great experience. Molke was located in the second district, across from Prater. This gave me easy access to the city (via the U1 and U2 lines) and the WU campus.

Transportation

Vienna's public transport system is 10/10. It is clean, reliable, safe, and affordable. A student semester pass (6 months) is around €75. Fines for not having a ticket are €100, so always have it on you and validated!

Culture

  • Viennese people have a reputation for being rude. As long as you speak and act with respect, you'll be given the same in return (most of the time).
  • Never jaywalk! Austrians take traffic rules seriously, they will yell at you in German.
  • Most places close on Sundays, so plan accordingly.

Basic German

  • Hallo = Hello
  • Tschüss = Bye
  • Bitte = Please
  • Danke = Thank you
  • Entschuldigung = Excuse me
  • Sprechen Sie Englisch? = Do you speak English?
